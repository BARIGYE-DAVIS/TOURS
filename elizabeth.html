QUEEN ELIZABETH NATIONAL PARK

About the park

Queen Elizabeth National Park, nestled in the heart of Uganda, is a breathtaking destination for wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers. Known for its diverse ecosystems, abundant wildlife, and stunning landscapes, this park offers an unforgettable experience.

The park was founded in 1952 as Kazinga National Park and renamed two years later to commemorate a visit by Queen Elizabeth II of England

The park is home to over 95 mammal species and over 600 bird species.

The Katwe explosion craters mark the park’s highest point at 1,350m above sea level, while the lowest point is at 910m, at Lake Edward.

Queen Elizabeth National Park is understandably Uganda’s most popular tourist destination. The park’s diverse ecosystems, which include sprawling savanna, shady, humid forests, sparkling lakes, and fertile wetlands, make it the ideal habitat for a classic big game, ten primate species including chimpanzees, and over 600 species of birds.

Set against the backdrop of the jagged Rwenzori Mountains, the park’s magnificent vistas include dozens of enormous craters carved dramatically into rolling green hills, panoramic views of the Kazinga Channel with its banks lined with hippos, buffalo, and elephants, and the endless Ishasha plains, whose fig trees hide lions ready to pounce on herds of unsuspecting Uganda kob.

As well as its outstanding wildlife attractions, Queen Elizabeth National Park has a fascinating cultural history. There are many opportunities for visitors to meet the local communities and enjoy storytelling, dance, music, and more. The gazetting of the park has ensured the conservation of its ecosystems, which in turn benefits the surrounding communities.

Most of Queen Elizabeth comprises open grassland and savannah which tends to be moist and woody in the west than the eastern part, Thorny Acacia dominates this savannah habitant with the high concentration of candelabra shrub a cactus that grows along the kazinga channel and on the kasenyi plains.

Highlights

The Big Five: Spot lions, elephants, buffaloes, leopards, and rhinoceroses in their natural habitats.

PrimatesEncounter chimpanzees, monkeys, and other fascinating primates in their lush forests

Birds: Discover over 600 species of birds, including the rare shoebill stork, in the park's diverse habitats.

Lakes: Explore the park's beautiful lakes, home to hippos, crocodiles, and a variety of bird species.

Crater Lakes: Visit the park's stunning crater lakes, formed by ancient volcanic activity, and enjoy the breathtaking views.

Kazinga Channel: Cruise along the Kazinga Channel and witness the park's wildlife up close, including elephants, hippos, and crocodiles.

Unique Experience

Queen Elizabeth National Park offers a wide range of experiences for visitors to enjoy. Whether you're interested in wildlife viewing, birdwatching, or simply exploring the park's stunning landscapes, there's something for everyone to enjoy.

Some of the activities you can enjoy in the park include:

Game Drives:Embark on a thrilling game drive through the park's savannah plains and spot a variety of wildlife, including lions, elephants, buffaloes, and more.

Boat Cruises:Take a boat cruise along the Kazinga Channel and enjoy close-up views of hippos, crocodiles, and a variety of bird species.

Chimpanzee Tracking:Embark on a guided trek to see chimpanzees in their natural habitat and learn about these fascinating primates.

Birdwatching:Discover the park's diverse birdlife, including the rare shoebill stork, on a guided birdwatching tour.

Nature Walks:Explore the park's lush forests and stunning landscapes on a guided nature walk and learn about the park's ecosystems and wildlife.

Cultural Visits:Visit local communities near the park and learn about their traditions, customs, and way of life.

Landscape and Geography

Queen Elizabeth National Park is known for its diverse landscapes and ecosystems, which include savannah plains, lush forests, crater lakes, and wetlands. The park is home to a wide variety of wildlife, including the famous Big Five – lions, elephants, buffaloes, leopards, and rhinoceroses – as well as a variety of bird species, primates, and other animals.

Savannas Experience the vast grasslands and acacia trees of the park's savanna ecosystems.

Forests Explore the park's lush forests, home to chimpanzees, monkeys, and other primates.

Lakes Relax by the shores of Lake George and Lake Edward, enjoying the serene atmosphere and stunning views

VISIT ISHASHA FOR TREE CLIMBING LIONS

There is this popular question, Do lion climb Trees? The answer is YES! Lions climb trees in Uganda! This happens in Queen Elizabeth National Park. The only other place in the world where you see this is in Lake Manyara National Park in Tanzania. Else where, lions do not climb trees

Ishasha is one of those tourist places which is very famous mostly for its tree climbing lions. Lions keep the whole day climbing trees or resting. Ishasha has a very beautiful sight you will find nowhere else in Africa. Most of them will be in the sycamore fig trees along with the acacia trees. These trees are big enough to offer as much support and shelter to the tree climbing lions as they need, especially in the rainy season. Enjoy a game viewing drive around Queen Elizabeth National Park as you take memorable moments on your camera and also see other animals that are inhabitants of this place like elephants, antelopes, and buffalos name it. One of the things you are most likely to find amazing in Ishasha is the fact that the manes of the male lions are black.
