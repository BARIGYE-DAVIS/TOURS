LAKE MBURO NATIONAL PARK

Lake Mburo National Park

Lake Mburo National Park is a compact gem, located conveniently close to the highway that connects Kampala to the parks of western Uganda. It is the smallest of Uganda’s savannah national parks and underlain by ancient Precambrian metamorphic rocks which date back more than 500 million years. It is home to 350 bird species as well as zebra, impala, eland, buffalo, oribi, Defassa waterbuck, leopard, hippo, hyena, topi and reedbuck.

Together with 13 other lakes in the area, Lake Mburo forms part of a 50km-long wetland system linked by a swamp. Five of these lakes lie within the park’s borders. Once covered by open savanna, Lake Mburo National Park now contains much woodland as there are no elephants to tame the vegetation. In the western part of the park, the savanna is interspersed with rocky ridges and forested gorges while patches of papyrus swamp and narrow bands of lush riparian woodland line many lakes.

At 260 square kilometers, Lake Mburo National Park is the smallest savannah national park in Uganda. The park has a variety of animals such as zebras, impalas, buffaloes, and over 300 bird species. The park is a great place to see the eland, impala, buffalo, oribi, Defassa waterbuck, leopard, hippo, hyena, topi and reedbuck. The park is also home to the largest population of zebras in Uganda. Animals

The park is home to 68 mammal species. Other animals include impala, warthogs, elands, buffaloes, oribi, Defassa waterbuck, leopard, hippo, hyena, topi and reedbuck. The park is also home to the largest population of zebras in Uganda, and other common herbivores include buffalo, hippopotamus, warthog, common eland, Rothschild’s giraffe, and waterbuck.

Explore Tourist Activities in Lake Mburo National Park

Discover a unique wildlife experience in Uganda's hidden gem.

1. Game Drives

A game drive in Lake Mburo is typically conducted in a 4x4 vehicle with an experienced park guide or ranger. The park is home to a wide variety of wildlife, especially known for its zebra populations, which you can encounter in large herds.

  • Zebras, giraffes, buffaloes, and antelope species
  • Predators like hyenas and jackals
  • Customizable drive times: 2 hours to a full-day safari

2. Boat Safari on Lake Mburo

A boat ride on Lake Mburo is a serene way to explore the park, especially around the papyrus swamps that line the lake's shores. During the ride, you’ll encounter hippos, crocodiles, and a wide variety of birdlife.

  • 1–2 hour boat ride with stunning views
  • Spot hippos, crocodiles, and waterfowl
  • Birdwatching opportunities: Shoebill stork, African fish eagle, and more

3. Birdwatching

Lake Mburo is home to over 350 bird species, making it a prime location for birdwatching. The park’s diverse habitats create a variety of microenvironments that attract different bird species.

  • Rare birds like the Shoebill stork and African fish eagle
  • Wetlands, savannah, and woodland habitats
  • Best for birdwatching from December to March and June to September

4. Walking Safaris

A walking safari allows you to experience the park in a more intimate way. Led by an armed ranger guide, you'll explore the environment up close and learn about the flora, fauna, and animal tracks.

  • Guided walks with expert rangers
  • Get closer to nature and observe smaller wildlife like bushbucks, warthogs, and birds
  • Typically 1-2 hours, often in the morning when animals are most active

5. Cultural Encounters

Lake Mburo lies in the heart of the Ankole region, famous for its Ankole cattle. Visitors can immerse themselves in the local culture through activities like traditional dances, cattle herding, and visiting homesteads.

  • Participate in traditional Ankole dances and music
  • Experience cattle herding and milking
  • Visit a homestead and learn about the pastoral lifestyle

6. Horseback Safaris

For a unique perspective, embark on a horseback safari through Lake Mburo. Horses offer a less intrusive way to approach wildlife, with close encounters with zebras, giraffes, and buffaloes.

  • 1-2 hour guided horseback safari
  • Ideal for both beginners and experienced riders
  • Approach wildlife quietly and peacefully

7. Night Game Drives

Explore the park after dark to spot nocturnal animals like bush babies, genets, and owls. The night game drive offers a whole new perspective on wildlife.

  • Spot nocturnal wildlife with the help of spotlights
  • See bush babies, civets, and other night creatures
  • 1-2 hour experience, usually after sunset
